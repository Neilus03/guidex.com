Abstract

Information Extraction (IE) systems are usually tailored to a single domain, which calls for expert schema design, manual annotation and expensive model re-training. Performance of Large Language Models drops sharply when label definitions drift. GUIDEX removes that bottleneck: it automatically builds domain-specific schemas, writes guidelines and produces synthetic instances. Fine-tuning LLaMa 3.1 on GUIDEX raises the state of the art on seven zero-shot NER benchmarks, gaining up to 7 F1 over the best previous method with no human labels, and nearly 3 F1 when combined with gold data. Models trained on GUIDEX better grasp complex, domain-specific annotation rules. Code, models and synthetic sets will be released.
